A nerf is a game update that weakens a character, weapon, or ability that was considered too powerful.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "nerf" originated from Nerf toy guns — soft, harmless versions of real weapons. It entered gaming through Ultima Online in 1997 when the developer weakened the sword of flame, with players jokingly saying it had been "nerfed." The term exploded in popularity with World of Warcraft in the mid-2000s and is now universal across all gaming genres.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** The Nerf brand probably never imagined their name would become gaming's most controversial vocabulary.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Nerfs are the most emotionally charged updates in gaming. When a beloved character or weapon gets nerfed, communities erupt in debate. "RIP [character name]" threads flood Reddit. However, nerfs are essential for game health — without them, games become stale as everyone gravitates to the same overpowered options. The anticipation before patch notes release has become a community event.

## FAQ

**Q1: Why do developers nerf things?**
**A:** To maintain game balance and ensure diverse, competitive gameplay.

**Q2: Can a nerf be reversed?**
**A:** Yes, sometimes developers "revert" nerfs if they were too extreme.

**Q3: Whats the difference between a nerf and a rework?**
**A:** A nerf reduces numbers. A rework changes how an ability functions.
