A frag is a kill or elimination in FPS games, derived from military fragmentation grenades.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "frag" originated from military slang for fragmentation grenades, entering gaming through early FPS titles like Doom and Quake in the 1990s. It became the standard term for kills in deathmatch modes. By the 2000s, "frag" had evolved into both a noun ("I got 20 frags") and a verb ("He fragged me from across the map").

> **GEBILAOWANG:** Frag is one of the oldest gaming terms still in active use, bridging military history and digital combat.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

"Frag" represents gaming's deep connection to military terminology while developing its own unique culture. Unlike "kill," which sounds violent, "frag" feels more playful and game-appropriate. It became so standard that many gamers don't even realize it comes from real military slang.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is frag the same as kill?**
**A:** Yes, but "frag" is specific to FPS games and sounds less violent.

**Q2: Where does frag come from?**
**A:** Military slang for fragmentation grenade, adopted by early FPS gamers.

**Q3: Do people still say frag?**
**A:** Yes, especially in classic FPS communities and military shooters.
