On paper, a glass cannon sounds like a terrible idea. Trade away all your defenses for damage? In practice, it's the most fun you can have dying in two hits.

## The Rise of the Glass Cannon

A glass cannon is a character, build, or playstyle that deals enormous damage but folds the moment anyone touches it. The image is right there in the name: a cannon made of glass. Devastating going out, fragile coming in.

Where did it come from? The term grew up with RPG build culture, back when stat allocation was a lifestyle choice. Put every point into offense and nothing into health or armor, and you got a character that deleted bosses and died to a strong breeze. Games like Diablo turned that trade-off into an art form, MOBAs turned it into a role description (marksmen and mages who need a whole team babysitting them), and by now the term shows up everywhere from fighting games to Pokémon. Yes, there's a famous purple ghost who's a glass cannon. Fans have done the math.

As a support main, I have a complicated relationship with the archetype. My job is keeping one alive. My [KDA](/slang/kda-gaming/) reflects how that usually goes.

## Why the Glass Cannon Hit Different

The glass cannon isn't just a build. It's a personality type. It attracts the player who would rather lose spectacularly than win carefully, who reads "high risk, high reward" and only processes the second half.

It also changed how games get designed. Developers build entire classes around the fantasy: the assassin who one-shots you and evaporates, the mage with no escape key. Balancing glass cannons is its own nightmare, because the archetype is only fun when the damage is genuinely scary and only fair when the fragility is genuinely punishing.

Here's the framework I hand every new player who wants to try one. I call it the two-hit rule: your job is ending the fight before the second hit lands on you. If you're regularly taking a second hit, the problem usually isn't the build. It's where you were standing when the fight started.

## FAQ

**Is a glass cannon the same as a tank?**

Total opposites. A tank sacrifices damage to absorb hits and protect the team. A glass cannon sacrifices survival to end fights fast. One wins by outlasting you, the other wins before you can respond. Most team compositions need both, which is why your tank gets grumpy when the glass cannon stands in front of them.

**Is a glass cannon good for beginners?**

Usually not. The build forgives nothing. It demands positioning, timing, and knowing exactly which fights you're allowed to take. New players tend to experience only the "glass" half of the deal. Learn on something sturdier, then come back when you understand why standing there was wrong.

**Is glass cannon a class or a build?**

Neither, technically. It's an archetype, a pattern that shows up across genres. Any class can be built as a glass cannon if the game lets you stack offense over defense. Some characters are born into it, some are built into it, and some players will force it onto literally anything. You know a [one-trick](/slang/one-trick-gaming/) like that. We all do.
