Griefing is intentionally ruining the game for your own teammates through sabotage, feeding, or disruptive behavior.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "griefing" originated from early MMOs like Ultima Online in the late 1990s, where players would deliberately disrupt others' gameplay for amusement. It expanded to all multiplayer genres by the 2000s. Modern games have sophisticated reporting systems specifically designed to combat griefing, with penalties ranging from temporary suspensions to permanent bans.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** Griefing is the antithesis of everything gaming communities strive for — it's pure toxicity disguised as fun.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Griefing represents the dark side of online anonymity. While some argue it's "just a game," the impact on teammates' mental health and enjoyment is real. Game developers have invested heavily in AI-driven detection systems to identify griefers automatically. The community has also become less tolerant, with "report for griefing" becoming a standard response to disruptive behavior.

## FAQ

**Q1: What's the difference between griefing and being bad?**
**A:** Intent. Griefing is deliberate sabotage. Being bad is lack of skill.

**Q2: Can you get banned for griefing?**
**A:** Yes, most games issue suspensions or permanent bans for repeated griefing.

**Q3: How do games detect griefing?**
**A:** Through player reports, AI behavior analysis, and statistical anomaly detection.
