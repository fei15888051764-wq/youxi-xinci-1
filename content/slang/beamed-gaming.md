Getting beamed means getting eliminated by a precise long-range shot with near-perfect tracking, often used in FPS games.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "beamed" emerged from Fortnite and Roblox communities around 2018, spreading to Call of Duty and other shooters by 2020. It describes being hit by a laser-accurate spray of bullets from distance. The term gained massive popularity through Twitch streamers and YouTube montages showcasing incredible tracking aim.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** The visual of a laser beam perfectly tracking a target makes this term incredibly descriptive.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Being beamed carries a mix of respect and frustration. On one hand, it acknowledges the opponent's incredible aim skill. On the other, it highlights the victim's helplessness against superior mechanics. The term became a badge of honor for skilled players and a meme for those on the receiving end.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is getting beamed the same as getting sniped?**
**A:** No — beamed refers to automatic weapon tracking, while sniped is a single precise shot.

**Q2: Can you beam someone with any weapon?**
**A:** Typically refers to ARs and SMGs with good tracking, not shotguns or snipers.

**Q3: Is beaming a compliment or insult?**
**A:** Both — it respects the aim skill while acknowledging you got destroyed.
