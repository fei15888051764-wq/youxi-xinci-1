Spend any time in a League or Overwatch subreddit and you'll see the word thrown around with a very specific mix of respect and dread.

A **one-trick** is a player who picks one character and basically never touches anything else. Hundreds of games, sometimes thousands, all on the same pick. The term comes from "one-trick pony," the circus animal with exactly one act, shortened over time to just "one-trick" or the acronym **OTP**.

## The Origin of the One-Trick

The phrase "one-trick pony" is way older than gaming. It described anyone with a single impressive skill and nothing behind it. Gaming adopted it once character-based games took over. MOBAs like League of Legends and Dota 2, then hero shooters like Overwatch, gave players rosters of dozens of characters, and some players looked at that menu and said: no thanks, I choose one.

The community shortened it fast. By the mid-2010s, "OTP" was standard vocabulary in champion select, and watching a one-trick hit Challenger with a single pick became its own genre of content.

## The One-Trick Effect

One-tricks warp every lobby they join. Teammates see the pick and either relax (this person has 2,000 games on that champion, we're fine) or panic (the enemy just banned it, we're doomed). That fear is rational: a one-trick whose character gets [nerfed](/slang/nerf-gaming/) has to rebuild their entire ranked identity from scratch.

But the upside is real. Playing one character forever builds muscle memory that flexible players can't touch: the one-trick isn't thinking about combos, so their whole brain is free for the [meta](/slang/meta-gaming/) and macro decisions. That's why a fed one-trick feels unstoppable and why so many of them climb further than "well-rounded" players ever do.

## One-Tricking in Real Conversations

"Respect. Deranged, but respect."

## FAQ

**Is a one-trick the same as a main?**

Not quite. A main is your favorite character: you play them most, but you can flex when the team needs it. A one-trick *can't* flex, or at least won't. Every one-trick has a main; not every main is a one-trick. The difference shows the second their pick gets banned.

**Do people still say one-trick in 2026?**

All the time, especially in ranked communities. The term's old enough that it barely registers as slang anymore; it's just how you describe that player type. You'll also see "OTP" used the same way in chat.

**Is one-tricking actually a good way to climb?**

Honestly? For a lot of players, yes. Consistency beats flexibility in solo queue, and nobody's more consistent than someone with a thousand games on one pick. The risk is real though: bans, counters, and patches can ruin your whole season. High reward, high blood pressure.
