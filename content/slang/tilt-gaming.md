Tilt is a state of mental frustration that leads to poor decision-making and decreased performance.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "tilt" originated from pinball machines in the mid-20th century — when players shook the machine too aggressively, it would activate a "tilt" sensor and lock the flippers. It entered poker slang in the 1990s to describe emotional play after a bad beat. Gaming adopted the term in the early 2000s through competitive poker players who also played video games.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** Tilt is the silent killer of ranked games — more LP is lost to tilt than to actual skill differences.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Tilt is one of the most studied psychological phenomena in gaming. Professional teams employ sports psychologists specifically to help players manage tilt. The concept has expanded beyond gaming into general productivity and mental health discussions. "Don't tilt" has become universal advice for anyone facing high-pressure situations.

## FAQ

**Q1: How do I know if Im tilted?**
**A:** Signs include: blaming teammates, making risky plays, playing longer than planned to "recover" losses.

**Q2: How do I stop tilting?**
**A:** Take breaks, mute chat, focus on your own gameplay, and remember it's just a game.

**Q3: Can tilt affect pro players?**
**A:** Absolutely. Even pros tilt — the difference is they recognize it faster and have coping strategies.
