On paper, WeirdChamp is just a badly cropped photo of a fighting game guy looking concerned. In practice, it's how Twitch chat says "I can't believe you just did that" without typing a single word.

## Using WeirdChamp Without Sounding Like a Bot

**Over in Twitch chat, streamer promises a "short" stream at hour nine:**

Streamer: "One more game and I'm done. Seriously."
Chat: "WeirdChamp"
Chat: "you said that 4 games ago WeirdChamp"
Chat: "WeirdChamp sure"

**Meanwhile on Discord:**

Sam: "guys I think I'm actually pretty good at this game"
Sam: *shares his 34% win rate*
Jules: "WeirdChamp"
Sam: "it's my teammates"
Jules: "WEIRDCHAMP"

Then there's the Reddit version. Somebody posts a take so bad the replies are just "WeirdChamp" forty times in a row. No argument. No rebuttal. Just the face. Honestly? More effective than a paragraph.

The golden rule: it's for disappointment, not tragedy. Whiffed plays, broken promises, bad takes. If the moment is genuinely sad, you want PepeHands instead. Different face, different funeral.

## The Origin of WeirdChamp

Where did WeirdChamp come from? It's a remix of a remix, which is about as Twitch as it gets. The face belongs to Ryan "Gootecks" Gutierrez, a Street Fighter community veteran and co-founder of Cross Counter TV. You already know his face even if you don't know his name. He was the original [PogChamp](/slang/pog-gaming/).

The expression comes from the same filming session. A cameraman tripped over a tripod, Gootecks pulled a face of pure secondhand disappointment, and the internet did what the internet does. On July 2nd, 2018, a Reddit user posted a screengrab of that grimace to r/forsen. Later that year a user named NyroLoL uploaded it to FrankerFaceZ as an emote, originally under the name "FeelsWeirdPog." The name got trimmed, BTTV picked it up, and then xQc's community adopted it so aggressively that the emote basically became his channel's second language. Spam in chat, daily memes on r/xQcOW, the works. By October 2019, StreamElements ranked it the 12th most popular emote on all of Twitch.

The part I love (as someone who mostly plays single-player games and watches this stuff like a nature documentary): the most famous version of this man's face is a fan edit of an outtake. Community archaeology at its finest.

After Gootecks' controversial posts in January 2021, Twitch removed his PogChamp and FrankerFaceZ dropped WeirdChamp too. BTTV never did. That's why it still floats around chat today.

## The WeirdChamp Effect

PogChamp was hype. WeirdChamp is doubt. Between those two faces, Twitch chat covers most of its emotional spectrum.

What makes it stickier than most emotes is the versatility. It works as a reaction, a verdict, and a prediction. Streamer announces a questionable build? WeirdChamp. Teammate says "trust me" before doing something illegal in spawn? WeirdChamp. Getting [jebaited](/slang/jebaited-gaming/) into believing the patch notes would fix your main? You already know.

It also carved out a niche that sarcasm emotes like [kappa](/slang/kappa-gaming/) can't fill. Kappa says "I'm joking." WeirdChamp says "I'm judging." Subtle difference, huge consequences.

Since nobody hands you a chart when you install BTTV, here's the one I wish I'd had:

| Emote | Emotional register | Deploy it when |
|---|---|---|
| PogChamp | hype, amazement | the play actually worked |
| WeirdChamp | disappointment, disbelief | the play should never have been attempted |
| PainsChamp | secondhand cringe | the play worked but you wish it hadn't |

## FAQ

**Is WeirdChamp the same as PogChamp?**

Same face, opposite energy. PogChamp is excitement when something incredible happens. WeirdChamp is disappointment when something questionable happens. They were both cut from the same Gootecks footage, which is why people confuse them. Think of them as twins who took very different life paths.

**When should I use WeirdChamp?**

When you're let down or calling nonsense. A streamer breaks a promise, a teammate makes a baffling call, someone posts a terrible take. Keep it playful. Spamming it at someone who's genuinely upset isn't a good look.

**Do people still use WeirdChamp?**

Yes, though the ground shifted. Twitch itself never hosted it, and FrankerFaceZ removed it in 2021 along with the other Gootecks-faced emotes. BTTV kept it, and communities like xQc's never stopped spamming it. It's one of those emotes that outlived its own controversy.

**How do I explain WeirdChamp to someone who doesn't watch Twitch?**

"It's a reaction image of a disappointed-looking man. People post it when someone does something dumb or breaks a promise. It means 'really?' in picture form." If they get reaction GIFs, they'll get this.
