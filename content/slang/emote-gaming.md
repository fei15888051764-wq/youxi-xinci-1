In gaming, an **emote** is a character animation or expression used to communicate. Ranges from dances to taunts to celebrations.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

Emotes originated in MMORPGs like *World of Warcraft* (2004), where players could /dance, /wave, and /laugh. *League of Legends* (2009) introduced champion-specific taunts and dances. *Fortnite* (2017) took emotes mainstream with licensed dances and the Battle Pass system. Twitch emotes (Kappa, PogChamp, MonkaS) created a parallel emote culture in streaming. By 2026, emotes are universal across gaming and streaming platforms.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** Emotes are the universal language of gaming — you don't need to speak the same language to understand a dance or a taunt.

## FAQ

**Q1: Can emotes be used during combat?**

Usually no — most games lock emotes during combat to prevent unfair distractions.

**Q2: Are emotes only in multiplayer games?**

Mostly, but some single-player games have gesture systems too.

**Q3: Why do people buy emotes?**

Expression, social bonding, and sometimes pure flex. A rare emote says "I was there."
