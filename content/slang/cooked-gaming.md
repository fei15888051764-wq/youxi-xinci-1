Being cooked means being completely destroyed, dominated, or outplayed in a game. A cooking metaphor for getting wrecked.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "cooked" emerged from African American Vernacular English (AAVE) and internet culture around 2022, entering gaming slang through Twitch chat and TikTok by 2024. The cooking metaphor (getting fried, roasted, cooked) resonated with gaming's competitive nature. By 2025, it had become mainstream Gen-Z gaming vocabulary.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** The cooking metaphor family (cooked, fried, roasted, burnt) shows how culinary destruction parallels gaming domination.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

"Cooked" represents a shift in gaming slang toward more creative and humorous descriptions of defeat. Unlike "rekt" or "owned," being cooked implies a slower, more thorough destruction. It's often used self-deprecatingly, making it less toxic than traditional trash talk.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is cooked worse than rekt?**
**A:** It's more thorough — "rekt" is instant, "cooked" implies sustained domination.

**Q2: Can you cook someone in a friendly way?**
**A:** Yes, it's often used playfully among friends without malice.

**Q3: What's worse than cooked?**
