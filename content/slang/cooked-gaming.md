In gaming, 'cooked' means being completely outplayed, defeated, or out of options. When someone says "you're cooked," they mean you've lost and there's no coming back.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

'Cooked' entered gaming slang from African American Vernacular English (AAVE) in the early 2020s, where it meant "finished" or "done for." It gained popularity through Twitch streamers and TikTok content creators around 2022-2023. By 2024, 'cooked' was standard gaming vocabulary, particularly among younger players. The term's rise coincides with the broader trend of AAVE-influenced gaming slang entering mainstream usage.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** The speed at which streamer slang enters mainstream gaming vocabulary is remarkable.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

'Cooked' is part of a new wave of gaming slang that emphasizes dramatic, entertaining ways to describe defeat. Unlike traditional terms like "owned" or "destroyed," "cooked" has a specific flavor — it implies not just losing, but being thoroughly and irreversibly defeated. The term also reflects the influence of streaming culture on gaming language. Streamers need catchy, dramatic vocabulary to entertain viewers, and "cooked" delivers.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is cooked the same as destroyed?**

Similar, but "cooked" implies being thoroughly and irreversibly defeated.

**Q2: Where did cooked come from?**

AAVE, popularized through Twitch streamers and TikTok.

**Q3: Is cooked offensive?**

No, it's playful banter. Much milder than traditional gaming insults.

**Q4: How do I explain cooked to a non-gamer in one sentence?**

"It means you're completely defeated with no way back — like being fully cooked, there's no uncooking."
