A buff is a game update that strengthens a character, weapon, or ability, making it more powerful or effective in gameplay.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "buff" originated from tabletop RPGs in the 1980s, describing temporary power boosts from spells or items. It migrated to video games with RPGs like EverQuest and World of Warcraft in the late 1990s. By the mid-2000s, "buff" and its counterpart "nerf" had become standard vocabulary across all gaming genres.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** Buff is the yin to nerfs yang — the eternal cycle of game balance.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Buffs represent hope for underpowered characters and weapons. When a beloved but weak character receives a buff, the community celebrates. However, overbuffing can lead to new balance issues, creating what players call "flavor of the month" picks. The anticipation of buffs in patch notes has become a community event.

## FAQ

**Q1: What's the difference between a buff and a rework?**
**A:** A buff increases numbers (damage, HP). A rework changes how an ability functions entirely.

**Q2: Can a buff make something too strong?**
**A:** Yes, overbuffing creates "OP" characters that dominate until nerfed.

**Q3: How often do buffs happen?**
**A:** Most games have monthly patches that include buffs and nerfs.
