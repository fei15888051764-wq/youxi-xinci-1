## Pop Culture Impact

Hopium is the internet's official term for believing in something that's obviously not happening. The meme format is Pepe or Wojak hooked to an oxygen tank labeled HOPIUM, huffing away like the copium tank's more optimistic sibling.

Gaming runs on the stuff. Every dead game's subreddit has players huffing hopium about a revival. Every fanbase before a showcase is passing the tank around — Half-Life 3, Bloodborne PC, your favorite MMO's comeback patch. Why do gamers say hopium so much? Because gamers are the most professionally disappointed audience on earth and they keep coming back anyway. Honestly, the tank never runs out.

## Origin & History

Hopium became slang after a 2010 Zero Hedge article accused Goldman Sachs of running on "hopium" — hope plus opium, a drug for people who refuse reality. It spread through finance Twitter and crypto communities, where holding a crashing coin required industrial doses.

The meme went visual in 2020 when Redditors started slapping oxygen tanks on Pepe, right in time for the US election to push it everywhere. By the early 2020s it was standard vocabulary across Reddit, Twitter, Discord, and Twitch chat. Where did hopium come from in gaming specifically? Straight from Twitch, where chat needed a word for streamers insisting the next game would be a W. Spoiler: it was not a W.

## FAQ

**Q: Is hopium the same as copium?**
A: Two stages of the same addiction. Hopium is before the disaster — believing it'll work out. Copium is after — explaining why the disaster was actually fine. First you huff hopium, then you huff copium, then you queue again.

**Q: When do you use hopium?**
A: Whenever someone's optimism has left the observable universe. Believing your hardstuck duo will suddenly carry, waiting on a sequel announced in 2017, trusting the patch notes will fix your main. All hopium.

**Q: Is hopium still used in 2026?**
A: Daily. It's baked into Twitch and Reddit vocabulary at this point, and every game showcase season brings the tank back out. The hopium meaning in gaming is basically a permanent mood.

**Q: What does hopium mean to a non-gamer?**
A: An imaginary drug you take to keep believing something that clearly won't happen. Sports fans waiting on a rebuild, anyone refreshing tracking on a late package — that's huffing hopium.
