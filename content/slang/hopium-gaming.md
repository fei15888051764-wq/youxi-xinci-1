The most dangerous substance in gaming isn't rage. It's hope.

## More Than Just a Word

Hopium is hope plus opium: clinging to optimism so irrational it might as well be a substance dependency. The meme version shows Pepe or Wojak huffing an oxygen tank labeled HOPIUM, blissed out while everything burns around them. The joke only works because it's confessional. You don't call someone else's hope hopium. You admit your own.

Gaming runs on the stuff. Believing the next patch will fix your main. Believing the sequel gets announced this year. Believing your 0/7 teammate will "lock in" for the last round. We are all, at some point, the frog on the tank. (If you've never huffed hopium over a roadmap, you've never loved a live-service game.)

It has a famous sibling: [copium](/slang/copium-gaming/), the drug for after the loss. Hopium is the before, copium is the after, and together they map the full emotional cycle of being a fan of anything.

## Hopium in Real Conversations

**Discord, two days before the patch notes:**

"They HAVE to buff our boy this time. The win rate data is right there."
"Pass the hopium."
"I'm just saying, the devs acknowledged the feedback."
"HUFFING. HE'S HUFFING AGAIN."

**Group chat, down 3-11 at half:**

"We can still come back. Their economy is fragile."
"How much hopium are you on right now"
"A responsible dose. A maintenance dose."

**Pre-release season, every year:**

"They've been quiet for eight months. That's a good sign, right? They're cooking."
Sir. That is pure, uncut hopium. And I would like some too.

## Before Hopium Was Everywhere

Where did hopium come from? Not gaming, actually. The earliest known use is a December 2010 Zero Hedge article, written under the pen name Tyler Durden (yes, the Fight Club one), accusing a Goldman Sachs forecast of running on "hopium" instead of data. Finance people kept the word alive for years.

It hit Urban Dictionary in 2018, then got its visual form in 2020 when a Redditor posted Pepe the Frog hooked up to a HOPIUM-labeled oxygen tank on r/pepethefrog. Crypto Twitter made it a daily word during the crashes, and by the early 2020s it had settled into Twitch chat, Reddit, and Discord as standard vocabulary. Fun trivia: the Wikipedia article for "Hopium" is about a French hydrogen car company. Nobody tell the meme.

My field guide to the whole pharmacy, since nobody ever labels the bottles:

| | Stage | What it sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Optimism | before the event, rational | "we have a real shot" |
| Hopium | before the event, delusional | "the devs will definitely fix it" |
| Copium | after the event, delusional | "the patch actually made us stronger" |

## FAQ

**Is hopium the same as copium?**

No, they're different stages of the same grief. Hopium is false hope before the outcome: "we can still win this." Copium is rationalization after the outcome: "losing was actually good for us." One denies the future, the other rewrites the past. Experienced fans cycle through both without noticing.

**Is calling something hopium an insult?**

Mostly no. It's usually self-deprecating, an admission that your hope is irrational and you're keeping it anyway. Aimed at someone else it's teasing at worst. Compared to what else chat could call you, hopium is practically affectionate. It's a [sadge](/slang/sadge-gaming/) kind of honesty.

**Where did the hopium tank image come from?**

The Pepe-on-an-oxygen-tank format was born on Reddit in mid-2020, in the Pepe the Frog subreddit of all places. The crypto community weaponized it during that year's chaos, and it spread from there. Now it lives rent-free in every Discord server on earth, next to the rest of your [rent free](/slang/rent-free-gaming/) collection.
