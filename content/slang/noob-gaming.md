A noob is a beginner or inexperienced player, often used as an insult in competitive games.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "noob" (also spelled "n00b" or "newb") originated from "newbie," a word dating back to the 1960s military and university slang for someone new to an environment. It entered gaming in the 1980s through BBS systems and early online games. By the 2000s, "noob" had evolved from a neutral descriptor to a widely used insult, with "newbie" retaining its gentler meaning while "noob" became the toxic variant.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** Noob is gaming's original insult — it predates even the most classic gaming slang.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

The evolution of "noob" from "newbie" reflects gaming's growing competitiveness and sometimes toxic culture. While calling someone a noob can be playful among friends, in random matchmaking it's often used to belittle. Some communities have actively pushed back, promoting "newbie" or "new player" as more welcoming alternatives. The term has also spawned endless memes and variations.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is noob the same as newbie?**
**A:** Originally yes, but now "noob" is insulting while "newbie" is more neutral/gentle.

**Q2: Can you get banned for calling someone a noob?**
**A:** Generally no, it's mild compared to other insults, but repeated targeted harassment can be reported.

**Q3: Whats worse than a noob?**
**A:** Terms like "hardstuck," "bot," or "feeder" are generally considered more insulting.
