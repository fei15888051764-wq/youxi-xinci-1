In gaming, 'lore' refers to the backstory, history, and world-building of a game — including character origins, historical events, relationships, and the overall narrative universe. Originally used in tabletop RPGs like Dungeons & Dragons, the term has become central to how players engage with story-rich games.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term 'lore' in gaming originated from tabletop role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons (1974) and early text-based RPGs, where it referred to the established history and mythology of the game world. As graphical RPGs emerged in the 1990s and 2000s, 'lore' became a central concept for players who wanted to deeply understand the game worlds they inhabited. The rise of Wikis in the mid-2000s created dedicated spaces for lore documentation. By the 2010s, 'lore' had expanded beyond RPGs into virtually all game genres. Even competitive games like League of Legends and Overwatch developed extensive lore. In 2026, 'lore' has also entered broader internet slang. Slangwise.com notes that it's now used to describe 'real-life drama or quirky events as if they were part of an epic saga.'

> **GEBILAOWANG:** This term is a perfect example of how gaming creates its own linguistic ecosystem.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Lore represents the storytelling dimension of gaming that goes beyond gameplay mechanics. For many players, understanding a game's lore is as important as mastering its mechanics. This creates a unique form of engagement where players become historians, archaeologists, and detectives within fictional worlds. The depth of modern game lore has also created specialized communities. 'Lore channels' on YouTube have millions of subscribers who watch hours-long videos analyzing game stories. This shows how gaming has evolved from pure entertainment into a medium for complex narrative experiences.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is lore the same as the main story?**
> Not exactly. The 'main story' is the narrative you experience while playing. 'Lore' includes the main story PLUS all the background history and world details.

**Q2: Which games have the best lore?**
> Games renowned for deep lore include: Dark Souls/Elden Ring, World of Warcraft, The Elder Scrolls, League of Legends, and Overwatch.

**Q3: Why do players care about lore?**
> Lore adds depth and meaning to gameplay. Understanding why characters fight and what the world has been through makes the game more emotionally engaging.

**Q4: How do I explain lore to a non-gamer in one sentence?**
> "It's the complete history and backstory of a game's world — like the Star Wars expanded universe, but for video games."
