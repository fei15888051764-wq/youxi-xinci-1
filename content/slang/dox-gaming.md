In gaming, **doxxing** (or **doxing**) is publishing someone's private information — real name, address, phone number, workplace — online without their consent. The nuclear option of online harassment.

---

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term **dox** originated in hacker culture in the 1990s, short for "documents" or "dropping docs" — releasing someone's private documents publicly. It entered gaming culture in the early 2010s as online gaming became more social and competitive. High-profile doxxing incidents involving streamers and pro players brought the term mainstream. By 2020, most gaming platforms had explicit anti-doxxing policies. In 2026, doxxing is universally condemned and often illegal, though it still happens in the most toxic corners of gaming culture.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Doxxing represents the darkest intersection of gaming and real-world harm. What starts as in-game trash talk can escalate to genuine threats against a person's physical safety. Several high-profile cases have resulted in **SWATing** (false emergency calls sending armed police to someone's home), job loss, and severe mental health impacts. The gaming industry has responded with stronger anti-harassment policies, but enforcement remains inconsistent. The cultural shift toward taking doxxing seriously — rather than dismissing it as "just online drama" — is one of gaming's most important developments. Communities that actively reject doxxing and protect members' privacy tend to be healthier and more welcoming.

**Swatting** — A dangerous escalation of doxxing where someone makes a false emergency report at the victim's address.
**Dox** — The act of publishing private information (verb) or the published information itself (noun).

## FAQ

**Q1: Is doxxing illegal?**

**A:** In many jurisdictions, yes. Laws vary by country, but publishing private information with malicious intent is often criminal harassment or stalking.

**Q2: What should I do if I'm being doxxed?**

**A:** 1) Document everything with screenshots. 2) Report to the platform. 3) Contact local authorities. 4) Inform your employer if workplace info is leaked. 5) Consider legal action.

**Q3: How can I protect myself from doxxing?**

**A:** Use unique usernames across platforms. Don't share personal details in public forums. Enable all privacy settings. Use a VPN. Be cautious about what you share, even in private messages.
