**"GG"** stands for **"Good Game"** — the most universally recognized term in gaming culture. It is typed or spoken at the end of a match as a gesture of sportsmanship, acknowledging the effort of both teams regardless of the outcome.

The term originated in the early days of online multiplayer gaming in the late 1990s and has since become a fundamental part of gaming etiquette across all platforms, genres, and competitive levels.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

### Early Online Gaming (1997–2004)

GG emerged from the need for quick, polite sign-offs in early multiplayer games like *StarCraft*, *Counter-Strike*, and *Quake*. Typing "good game" took too long in post-match lobbies, so players abbreviated it to "gg."

### MMO Era (2004–2012)

Massively multiplayer games like *World of Warcraft* normalized GG as standard etiquette. Battleground matches, dungeon runs, and raid encounters all concluded with GG.

### Esports Era (2012–2026)

Professional gaming made GG a ceremonial tradition:
- Pro players shake hands and say GG after matches
- Tournament brackets display "GG" in elimination announcements
- Casters use "GG" to call the end of a game

## Socio-Cultural Gain

GG represents the **social contract of competitive gaming**: compete fiercely, but respect your opponent. In a space often criticized for toxicity, GG is a small but powerful force for sportsmanship.

### GG Etiquette

| Timing | Appropriate? |
|--------|-------------|
| End of match | ✅ Standard |
| Mid-match (winning) | ❌ Disrespectful |
| After close game | ✅ Especially meaningful |

### Variations

- **GG WP** — "Good Game, Well Played" (extra respectful)
- **GG EZ** — "Good Game, Easy" (taunting, considered toxic)
- **GG NO RE** — "Good Game, No Rematch" (playful)
- **GGEZ** — Spam meme in Twitch chat

## FAQ

**Q1: Is saying GG after winning disrespectful?**

Depends on context. In casual games, mutual GG is standard. In some competitive scenes, GG should come from the loser first.

**Q2: What does GG EZ mean?**

"Good Game, Easy" — used to taunt opponents. Many games auto-replace it with self-deprecating messages.

**Q3: Can GG be used sarcastically?**

Yes. A "gg" after your teammate makes a terrible play carries very different meaning than one at the end of a close match.
