Pog is an expression of extreme excitement or hype, originating from the PogChamp Twitch emote.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "Pog" originated from the PogChamp emote on Twitch in 2012, featuring fighting game player Ryan Gutierrez's surprised face. The emote became synonymous with excitement and hype moments. By 2017, "Pog" and "Poggers" had spread beyond Twitch to all gaming communities. In 2021, Twitch removed the original PogChamp emote due to controversy, replacing it with a new face, but the term "Pog" remained deeply embedded in gaming culture.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** PogChamp's journey from emote to universal slang is a masterclass in how Twitch shapes gaming language.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

"Pog" represents the deep integration of streaming culture into gaming vocabulary. It's one of the few terms that works equally well in text, voice, and as an emote. The term has become so ubiquitous that even non-gamers recognize it. Its staying power after the original emote's removal proves that language evolves beyond its origins.

## FAQ

**Q1: What happened to the original PogChamp emote?**
**A:** It was removed in 2021 due to the player's controversial tweets, but new versions exist.

**Q2: Is Pog only for Twitch?**
**A:** No, it's used everywhere in gaming — Discord, in-game chat, even verbal communication.

**Q3: Whats the difference between Pog and Poggers?**
**A:** "Poggers" is more exaggerated and meme-y. "Pog" is the standard form.
