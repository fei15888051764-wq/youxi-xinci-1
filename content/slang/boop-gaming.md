In gaming, 'boop' refers to an ability that gently pushes or displaces an enemy, often off a ledge or into danger. The term was popularized by Overwatch's Lucio, who shouts "Boop!" when using his Soundwave ability.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term 'boop' existed in English as a playful onomatopoeia long before gaming, but its gaming-specific meaning emerged with Overwatch in 2016. Lucio's Soundwave ability, which pushes enemies backward, was accompanied by the voiceline "Boop!" — and the community immediately adopted it. By 2017, 'boop' was standard Overwatch vocabulary for any displacement ability. The term spread to other games with similar mechanics: *Paladins*, *Apex Legends*, and even *Super Smash Bros.* all have 'boop' abilities. In 2026, 'boop' is recognized across gaming as a friendly term for "pushing someone in a funny way."

## Socio-Cultural Gain

'Boop' stands out in gaming slang for being genuinely wholesome. Unlike most gaming terms which describe violence, competition, or toxicity, 'boop' is playful and lighthearted. The term also demonstrates how character voicelines can become community vocabulary. When a character says something catchy, players adopt it. This has happened with many games: *Hearthstone*'s "The pleasure is mine," *League of Legends*' "OK," and countless others. 'Boop' has transcended gaming into general internet culture. People use it for gentle, playful interactions — booping a cat's nose, booping a friend's screen. This crossover shows gaming's influence on everyday language.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is boop only from Overwatch?**

Popularized by it, but used for similar abilities in other games.

**Q2: What makes a boop different from a knockback?**

Boop is playful and gentle-sounding. Knockback is the mechanical term.

**Q3: Can any character boop?**

Any character with a displacement ability can technically boop.

**Q4: How do I explain boop to a non-gamer in one sentence?**

"It's when you gently push an enemy off a cliff in a video game, usually while shouting 'boop!'"
