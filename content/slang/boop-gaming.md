In gaming, 'boop' refers to an ability that gently pushes or displaces an enemy, often off a ledge or into danger. The term was popularized by Overwatch's Lucio, who shouts "Boop!" when using his Soundwave ability.

---

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term 'boop' existed in English as a playful onomatopoeia long before gaming, but its gaming-specific meaning emerged with Overwatch in 2016. Lucio's Soundwave ability, which pushes enemies backward, was accompanied by the voiceline "Boop!" — and the community immediately adopted it. By 2017, 'boop' was standard Overwatch vocabulary for any displacement ability. The term spread to other games with similar mechanics: *Paladins*, *Apex Legends*, and even *Super Smash Bros.* all have 'boop' abilities. In 2026, 'boop' is recognized across gaming as a friendly term for "pushing someone in a funny way."

## Socio-Cultural Gain

'Boop' occupies a unique place in gaming culture as one of the few genuinely wholesome terms. Unlike aggressive slang like 'pwned' or 'rekt,' booping is seen as playful mischief rather than dominance. Environmental kills via booping have become their own genre of gaming clips, with millions of views on TikTok and YouTube. The term has even transcended gaming into general internet culture, where 'boop' describes any gentle, playful push or poke. In 2026, the boop remains a beloved mechanic that turns competitive games into moments of joy.

## FAQ

**Q1: Which characters can boop?**

Lucio (Overwatch), Pharah (Overwatch), Brigette (Overwatch), and many heroes in *Paladins* and *Apex Legends* have boop-like abilities.

**Q2: Is booping considered toxic?**

Generally no. Most players see it as funny. However, excessive targeting of one player with boops can be seen as harassment.

**Q3: What's the best map for booping?**

Any map with environmental hazards (cliffs, water, lava). Overwatch's Ilios and Lijiang Tower are famous for boop plays.
