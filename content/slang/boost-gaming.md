In gaming, **boost** can mean three different things: a temporary power-up, an XP multiplier, or paying someone to rank up your account. Context matters.

---

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term 'boost' entered gaming from motorsports in the early 2000s, where it referred to temporary speed increases. In RPGs like World of Warcraft (2004), 'boost' described temporary stat enhancements from spells or items. The negative meaning — account boosting — emerged with ranked competitive games in the 2010s. Games like *League of Legends* and *Overwatch* made boosting a widespread issue, leading to most publishers banning the practice by 2018. In 2026, 'boost' remains one of gaming's most context-dependent terms.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Boost culture represents one of gaming's most contentious issues. While temporary boosts (power-ups, XP multipliers) are universally accepted gameplay mechanics, account boosting undermines competitive integrity. The practice has created an underground economy, with boosting services advertising on Discord and social media. Some players defend boosting as a way for busy adults to access high-level content, while others see it as cheating that ruins matchmaking. In 2026, most major publishers actively combat boosting through detection algorithms and permanent bans.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is account boosting illegal?**

Not legally, but it violates the Terms of Service of virtually every competitive game. Getting caught results in permanent bans.

**Q2: What's the difference between boosting and carrying?**

Carrying is when a skilled player helps their team win through legitimate gameplay. Boosting is when someone else plays on your account to artificially raise your rank.

**Q3: Are XP boosts worth buying?**

Depends on your play style. If you're grinding levels actively, they save time. For casual players, the value is questionable.
