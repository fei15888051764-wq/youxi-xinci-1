**"Pocket"** in gaming describes the closest partnership in team-based competitive play. When a support player decides to dedicate all their healing, buffs, and attention to one specific teammate — usually the carry — that's called **pocketing**. It's the gaming equivalent of having a personal bodyguard who also happens to keep you alive.

## What Does "Pocket" Mean?

To **pocket** someone means to focus all your support resources on a single teammate. The "pocket" refers to staying close to that player, keeping them healed, buffed, and protected while they deal damage or make plays.

## How Pocketing Works

**The pocket (support):**
- Prioritizes healing/buffing one player over the team
- Stays positioned near their pocket target
- Uses defensive abilities to protect them
- Sacrifices team-wide utility for single-target power

**The pocketed (carry):**
- Receives disproportionate resources
- Expected to carry the damage/load
- Has survivability beyond normal levels
- Becomes the team's primary win condition

## When Pocketing Works

- **The carry is skilled** — Resources aren't wasted
- **Clear communication** — Both players coordinate
- **Enemy can't focus** — They can't dive the backline
- **Team accepts it** — Others play self-sufficiently

## When Pocketing Fails

- **The carry is bad** — Wasted resources, lost team fights
- **The rest of the team dies** — Not enough healing to go around
- **Enemy focuses the pocket** — Kill the support, kill the strategy
- **Tilt from teammates** — "Heal me too!" "Pocket me!"

## Related Terms

- **Hard pocket** — Extreme dedication, almost ignoring the team
- **Soft pocket** — Prioritizing one player but still helping others
- **Pocket healer** — Support focused entirely on one target
- **Carry** — The player typically being pocketed
- **Peel** — Protecting the support so they can keep pocketing

## The Bottom Line

Pocketing is a legitimate strategy when executed well, but it's also a source of endless team drama. The key is knowing when to commit to a pocket and when to spread your support love to the whole team.

A good support knows how to pocket. A great support knows when to stop.
