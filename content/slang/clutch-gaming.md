A clutch is winning a round or match while under extreme pressure, often alone against multiple opponents. The most celebrated play in competitive gaming.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term "clutch" originated from sports commentary in the mid-20th century, describing athletes who performed under pressure. It entered gaming through Counter-Strike in the early 2000s, where 1vX situations were common. The term exploded in popularity with the rise of esports and streaming in the 2010s.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** Clutch moments are what make esports worth watching — pure adrenaline and skill under pressure.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

Clutch plays represent the pinnacle of gaming skill and mental fortitude. They're the moments that get clipped, shared, and remembered. The term has become so ubiquitous that "clutch" is used outside gaming to describe any high-pressure success. Some players develop a reputation as "clutch players" who consistently deliver in critical moments.

## FAQ

**Q1: What makes a play a clutch?**
**A:** High-pressure situation (low HP, outnumbered, game on the line) + successful outcome.

**Q2: Can you clutch in casual games?**
**A:** Yes, though the term is most commonly used in competitive/ranked modes.

**Q3: Whats a clutch factor?**
**A:** A player's demonstrated ability to consistently perform well in high-pressure situations.
