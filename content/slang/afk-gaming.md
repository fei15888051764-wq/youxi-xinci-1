AFK stands for "Away From Keyboard" and is used to indicate a player is temporarily unavailable or has stepped away from their computer during a game.

---

## Trajectory & Chronology

The term AFK emerged from early internet chat rooms and MMO gaming in the late 1990s. Players needed a quick way to inform teammates they were stepping away without disconnecting. The acronym spread rapidly through multiplayer games like EverQuest and World of Warcraft. By the mid-2000s, it had become standard vocabulary across all gaming genres. The term evolved beyond literal keyboard absence to represent any temporary unavailability, including console players saying AFK even when using controllers.

> **GEBILAOWANG:** AFK is one of the oldest and most practical terms in gaming vocabulary.

## Socio-Cultural Gain

AFK represents the practical reality of shared gaming spaces — people need bathroom breaks, doorbells ring, and pets demand attention. Unlike rage-quitting, going AFK acknowledges social responsibility to teammates. However, extended AFK periods became a significant pain point in ranked competitive gaming, leading to AFK detection systems and automated penalties in modern games.

## FAQ

**Q1: How long is considered acceptable to be AFK?**
**A:** In casual games, 2-3 minutes is usually fine if announced. In ranked, any AFK is unacceptable.

**Q2: Can I get banned for being AFK?**
**A:** Yes, most competitive games have AFK detection that issues temporary bans for repeated offenses.

**Q3: What's the difference between AFK and DC?**
**A:** AFK means you're still connected but away. DC (Disconnected) means you lost connection entirely.
